5.1. Spherical harmonic vectors#

Starting from the spherical harmonics, we can define the three spherical harmonics vectors

(5.1)#\[\begin{split} \begin{align} & \mathbf{P}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi) = \hat{\mathbf{r}}\,Y_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi) \\ & \mathbf{B}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi) = \frac{x}{\gamma_n}\,\boldsymbol{\nabla} Y_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi) = \frac{1}{\gamma_n}\,\left(\hat{\boldsymbol{\vartheta}}\,\frac{\partial Y_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)}{\partial \theta} + \hat{\boldsymbol{\varphi}}\,\csc\vartheta\,\frac{\partial Y_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)}{\partial \varphi}\right) \\ & \mathbf{C}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi) = \mathbf{B}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\times\hat{\mathbf{r}} = \frac{\boldsymbol{\nabla}\times(x\,\mathbf{P}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi))}{\gamma_n} = \frac{1}{\gamma_n}\,\left(\hat{\boldsymbol{\vartheta}}\,\csc\vartheta\,\frac{\partial Y_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)}{\partial \varphi} - \hat{\boldsymbol{\varphi}}\,\frac{\partial Y_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)}{\partial \vartheta}\right) \end{align} \end{split}\]

with

(5.2)#\[ \gamma_n=\sqrt{n\,(n+1)} \]

Similar to the spherical harmonics, which can be used for representing scalar functions over the sphere, the spherical harmonic vectors can be used for representing vector fields

(5.3)#\[ \begin{align} & \mathbf{f}(\vartheta,\varphi) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty\sum_{m=-n}^n \big(f_{n,m}^P\,\mathbf{P}_{n}^m(\vartheta,\varphi)+ f_{n,m}^B\,\mathbf{B}_{n}^m(\vartheta,\varphi)+f_{n,m}^C\,\mathbf{C}_{n}^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\big) \end{align} \]

where \(f_{n,m}^X\) (with \(X=P,B\) or \(C\)) are the spherical harmonic coefficients. We note that \(\mathbf{B}_{0,0}=\mathbf{C}_{0,0}=\mathbf{0}\) because \(Y_0^0=1\) does not depend on the angular coordinates \((\vartheta,\varphi)\). In this respect, the spherical harmonic coefficients \(f_{0,0}^B\) and \(f_{0,0}^C\) have no meaning and, by convenction, we set them to zero

(5.4)#\[ \begin{align} & f_{0,0}^B = f_{0,0}^C = 0 \end{align} \]

Like the spherical harmonics, the spherical harmonic vectors are orthogonal in the following senses

(5.5)#\[ \int_{\mathcal{\Omega}} \mathbf{P}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\cdot\mathbf{P}_p^q(\vartheta,\varphi)\,\mathrm{d} \Omega = 4\,\pi\,\delta_{n,p}\,\delta_{m,q} \qquad \int_{\mathcal{\Omega}} \mathbf{B}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\cdot\mathbf{B}_p^q(\vartheta,\varphi)\,\mathrm{d} \Omega = 4\,\pi\,\delta_{n,p}\,\delta_{m,q} \qquad \int_{\mathcal{\Omega}} \mathbf{C}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\cdot\mathbf{C}_p^q(\vartheta,\varphi)\,\mathrm{d} \Omega = 4\,\pi\,\delta_{n,p}\,\delta_{m,q} \]
(5.6)#\[ \mathbf{P}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\cdot\mathbf{B}_p^q(\vartheta,\varphi) = 0 \qquad \mathbf{P}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\cdot\mathbf{C}_p^q(\vartheta,\varphi) = 0 \qquad \int_{\mathcal{\Omega}} \mathbf{B}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\cdot\mathbf{C}_p^q(\vartheta,\varphi)\,\mathrm{d} \Omega = 0 \]

and, so, we can obtain the spherical harmonic coefficients of a vector field as follows

(5.7)#\[ f_{n,m}^X = \frac{1}{4\,\pi}\int_{\mathcal{\Omega}} \mathbf{f}(\vartheta,\varphi)\cdot\mathbf{X}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\,\mathrm{d} \Omega \]

A useful formalism

By noting that

(5.8)#\[\begin{split} \frac{\partial \mathrm{tri}_{m}(\varphi)}{\partial \varphi} = m\,\mathrm{tri}_{-m}(\varphi) = \begin{cases} m\,\sin(-m\,\varphi) = -m\,\sin(m\,\varphi) & m < 0 \\ m\,\cos(-m\,\varphi) = m\,\cos(m\,\varphi) & m\geq 0 \end{cases} \end{split}\]

with \(\mathrm{tri}_m\) given by eq. (4.30), the derivative of the spherical harmonics with respect to the longitude become

(5.9)#\[ \frac{\partial Y_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)}{\partial \varphi} = m\,P_n^{\mid m \mid}(\cos\theta)\,\mathrm{tri}_{-m}(\varphi) = m\,Y_n^{-m}(\vartheta,\varphi) \]

In this respect, the spherical harmonic vectors \(\bb{B}_n^m\) and \(\bb{C}_n^m\) yields

(5.10)#\[\begin{split} \begin{align} & \mathbf{B}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi) = \frac{1}{\gamma_n}\,\left(\hat{\boldsymbol{\vartheta}}\,\frac{\partial Y_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)}{\partial \theta} + m\,\hat{\boldsymbol{\varphi}}\,\csc\vartheta\,Y_n^{-m}(\vartheta,\varphi)\right) \\ & \mathbf{C}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi) = \frac{1}{\gamma_n}\,\left(\hat{\boldsymbol{\vartheta}}\,\csc\vartheta\,m\,Y_n^{-m}(\vartheta,\varphi) - \hat{\boldsymbol{\varphi}}\,\frac{\partial Y_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)}{\partial \vartheta}\right) \end{align} \end{split}\]

5.1.1. Basic properties#

In the following we shall consider the following expressions for the divergence and the curl of the spherical harmonic vectors multiplied by a function \(f\) that depends only on the radial distance \(x\) yield

(5.11)#\[\begin{split} \begin{align} &\boldsymbol{\nabla}\cdot\big(f(x)\,\mathbf{P}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\big)= \left(\dot{f}(x)+\frac{2}{x}\,f(x)\right)\,Y_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi) \\ & \boldsymbol{\nabla}\cdot\big(f(x)\,\mathbf{B}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\big) = -\frac{f(x)}{x}\,\gamma_n\,Y_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi) \\ &\boldsymbol{\nabla}\cdot\big(f(x)\,\mathbf{C}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\big) = 0 \end{align} \end{split}\]

and

(5.12)#\[\begin{split} \begin{align} & \boldsymbol{\nabla}\times\big(f(x)\,\mathbf{P}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\big)= \gamma_n\,\frac{f(x)}{r}\,\mathbf{C}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi) \\ &\boldsymbol{\nabla}\times\big(f(x)\,\mathbf{B}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\big) = -\left(\dot{f}(x)+\frac{f(x)}{x}\right)\,\mathbf{C}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi) \\ & \boldsymbol{\nabla}\times\big(f(x)\,\mathbf{C}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\big) = \gamma_n\,\frac{f(x)}{r}\,\mathbf{P}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi) + \left(\dot{f}(x) + \frac{f(x)}{x}\right)\,\mathbf{B}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi) \end{align} \end{split}\]

5.1.2. Identities#

In the following, we shall consider the following identities for the integrals of the spherical harmonic vector over the unit sphere

(5.13)#\[ \int_{\Omega}\mathbf{P}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\,\mathrm{d}\Omega = \frac{4\,\pi}{\sqrt{3}}\,\mathbf{p}_m\,\delta_{n,1} \qquad \int_{\Omega}\mathbf{B}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\,\mathrm{d}\Omega = \frac{4\,\pi\,\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{3}}\,\mathbf{p}_m\,\delta_{n,1} \qquad \int_{\Omega}\mathbf{C}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\,\mathrm{d}\Omega =\mathbf{0} \]

with

(5.14)#\[ \bb{p}_0 = \bn{x}_3 \qquad \bb{p}_{-1} = \bn{x}_1 \qquad \bb{p}_1 = \bn{x}_2 \]

We note also that

(5.15)#\[ \bb{P}_1^m(\vartheta,\varphi)+ \gamma_1\,\bb{B}_1^m = \bb{p}_m \qquad Y_1^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\,\bb{p}_m = \bn{r}(\vartheta,\varphi) \]

Additional usefull identities are

(5.16)#\[ \int_{\Omega}\mathbf{P}_n^m(\vartheta,\varphi)\,Y_{k,q}(\vartheta,\varphi)\,\mathrm{d}\Omega = \frac{4\,\pi}{\sqrt{3}}\,\mathbf{p}^n_{m,q}\,\delta_{n,k+1} \]

with \(\bb{p}^n_{m,q}\) being constant vectors with \(m=-n,\cdots,n\) and \(q=-(n-1),\cdots,n-1\) at fixed \(n\).
